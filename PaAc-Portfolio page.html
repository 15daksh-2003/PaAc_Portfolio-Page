A Way To Entangle Photons, Naturally?

Because photons also possess angular momentum and polarization (hence why polarized glasses work the way they do), when they are created inside a spinning black hole’s accretion disk they can become entangled to the black hole itself.

The strength of this entanglement depends on how “quickly” the rotating black hole “spins”; in other words, how much spacetime near the black hole has been twisted in the same direction as its direction of physical spin.
